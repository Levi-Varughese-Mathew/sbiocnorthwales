Parumala Thirumeni

St. Gregorios of Parumala, fondly known as Parumala Thirumeni, was the first canonized saint of the Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church. Born in 1848 in Mulanthuruthy, Kerala, he exhibited a deep love for prayer and spirituality from a young age. He became a deacon at the age of 10 and a priest by 18, dedicating his life to asceticism, fasting, and spiritual discipline. Recognized for his wisdom and devotion, he was consecrated as a bishop at just 28 years old, becoming the youngest bishop in the history of the Malankara Church. Parumala Thirumeni was a gifted orator, a scholar, and a spiritual guide, known for his commitment to educating the faithful.

St Mary

St. Mary, also known as Theotokos (Mother of God), holds a unique and sacred place in Orthodox Christianity. She was chosen by God to bear Jesus Christ, the Savior of the world, making her the most blessed among women (Luke 1:42). She is honored for her purity, humility, and obedience, accepting God's will despite the challenges she faced. Throughout Jesus' life, St. Mary remained a pillar of strength and faith, witnessing His ministry, crucifixion, and resurrection. In the Orthodox Church, she is considered the greatest intercessor, continuously praying for the faithful. Her life serves as an example of unwavering faith, and her Dormition (Assumption) is a major feast in the Church, celebrating her entrance into heaven. Orthodox Christians seek her prayers, believing that as the Mother of God, she has a special place in interceding for humanity.

St Behnam

St. Behnam was a 4th-century prince of Assyria, known for his martyrdom and unwavering faith. He and his sister St. Sarah were converted to Christianity by St. Matthew the Hermit, despite coming from a non-Christian royal family. Their newfound faith, however, led to persecution by their father, King Sinharib, who ultimately ordered their execution. Before his death, St. Behnam remained steadfast in his commitment to Christ, refusing to renounce his beliefs. His martyrdom is a powerful testament to courage, devotion, and sacrifice for one's faith. Over time, a monastery was built in his honor, becoming a significant place of worship and pilgrimage in the Orthodox tradition.Today, St. Behnam's life continues to inspire believers, reminding them of the strength found in faith and the eternal reward of those who remain devoted to God. His story is a symbol of the triumph of Christianity even in the face of persecution.
