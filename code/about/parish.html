ABOUT THE PARISH

St Behanans Indian Orthodox Church

The Church Father of St. Behanan's Indian Orthodox Church is St. Behnam (Mar Behnam), after whom the church is named. St. Behnam is a venerated saint in the Syriac Christian tradition, including the Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church (Indian Orthodox Church), which has its roots in the Oriental Orthodox tradition.

Who is St. Behnam?

St. Behnam was a 4th-century prince from Assyria, who, along with his sister St. Sarah, converted to Christianity through the influence of St. Matthew the Hermit. After their conversion, they were martyred by their father, King Sinharib, for refusing to renounce their newfound Christian faith. St. Behnam is remembered as a martyr and a witness to Christ's teachings, and his story inspires faith and courage among Christians. He is venerated as a saint in the Syriac Christian tradition, particularly among the Assyrian Church of the East, the Syriac Orthodox Church, and the Chaldean Catholic Church. His feast day is celebrated on August 10th.

Significance of St. Behnam in the Indian Orthodox Church:

The Indian Orthodox Church, being part of the Oriental Orthodox communion, honours saints like St. Behnam who are prominent in the Syriac Christian tradition.

St. Behnam is venerated as a saint in the Indian Orthodox Church, and his name is associated with several parishes and institutions within the Church. St. Behanan's Indian Orthodox Church is one such parish that bears his name and seeks to honour his memory and witness to the faith.
